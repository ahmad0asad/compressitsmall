How it works

This tool renders each PDF page on your device using PDF.js, then exports every page as a JPEG image and packages the results into a single ZIP for download. The original PDF never leaves your browser.

  • Scale controls image sharpness. Higher scale produces clearer images but uses more memory.
  • Quality controls JPEG size. Lower values reduce file size but can introduce artifacts.
  • For scanned PDFs, a higher scale + medium quality usually delivers the best readability.

PDF to JPG: Convert PDF Pages to Image Files

Sometimes you need a PDF as an image — to embed in a presentation, share via messaging app, upload to a form that only accepts images, or create a thumbnail preview. This tool converts each page of your PDF into a high-quality JPG image, directly in your browser.

How to convert PDF to JPG

  1. Upload your PDF.
  2. Choose the output quality — higher quality means larger file size; medium quality is suitable for screen viewing and most form uploads.
  3. Click Convert — each page becomes an individual JPG file.
  4. Download — save each image or download all as a zip.

When to convert PDF to JPG

  • Online forms that require images — Many government and institutional forms accept JPG/PNG but not PDF.
  • WhatsApp and messaging — Images share and preview more reliably than PDFs in messaging apps.
  • Website and social media — PDF content embedded in posts or pages must be converted to image format first.
  • Slide deck inserts — Import PDF charts or diagrams into PowerPoint or Google Slides as images.
  • Document thumbnails — Create a preview image of the first page for use in listings or galleries.

JPG vs PNG for converted PDFs

JPG is better for PDFs with photos, gradients, and complex graphics — it compresses well and produces smaller files. PNG is better for PDFs with line art, text, or diagrams, because PNG is lossless and preserves sharp edges. Use our PDF to PNG tool if your PDF contains mainly text or line graphics.

Frequently asked questions

What resolution are the output images?
Standard quality outputs at approximately 150 DPI — suitable for screen viewing and most online uploads. High quality outputs at 200–300 DPI for print-ready images.
Will text in the PDF be readable in the JPG?
Yes, as long as the original PDF has clear, sharp text. If the source is a scanned PDF with poor original quality, the JPG output will reflect that — garbage in, garbage out.
Can I convert only specific pages?
Yes — specify page numbers before converting to process only the pages you need.
Is my file kept private?
All processing is local. Your PDF is never sent to our servers.
