Compress PDF: Reduce File Size Without Limits

Large PDF files get rejected by email servers, university portals, visa platforms, and cloud storage — often with no helpful explanation. This browser-based compressor re-renders every page at your chosen resolution and re-encodes it as a compressed image, guaranteeing a smaller output for every PDF type including scanned documents.

How to compress a PDF

  1. Drop your PDF into the upload area or click to browse.
  2. Choose a compression level — Medium works for most documents.
  3. Click Compress — each page is processed locally in your browser.
  4. Download the compressed file and check the size saving.

Common use cases

  • University and scholarship submissions — Most portals cap uploads at 2–5 MB.
  • Visa and embassy applications — Supporting documents limited to 1–2 MB per file.
  • Job application portals — HR systems often reject files over 5 MB.
  • Email attachments — Pre-compress before sending via Gmail or Outlook.
  • Scanned documents — Re-compress at lower DPI for dramatic size reduction.
