Real Upload Limits in 2026: Email, Scholarships, University Portals in Spain and Italy

Exact size limits and format requirements for the most common Spanish and Italian university portals, government systems, and scholarship applications used by international students.

International students applying to universities in Spain and Italy consistently report that document upload rejections are one of the most stressful parts of the application process. The limits are strict, the error messages are often vague, and the systems are not always consistent between institutions. This guide compiles the actual limits and requirements for the most commonly used portals so you can prepare your documents correctly from the start.

Italian university portals and scholarship systems

Italy's higher education system uses several different platforms depending on the institution and the type of application.

Universitaly is the centralised portal used by many Italian universities for international student pre-enrolment. The system accepts PDF files only and enforces a limit of between 1MB and 2MB per document depending on the specific field. Some institutions have configured their Universitaly integration to accept up to 5MB, but 2MB is the safe working assumption for any document you cannot confirm. All documents must be PDF, not scanned as JPEG or PNG and uploaded directly.

ESSE3 is the student management system used by many Italian public universities for enrolment and document submission after admission. ESSE3 implementations vary by institution but commonly set a 5MB limit per attachment. The system rejects password-protected PDFs and may reject files produced by certain PDF creation workflows. If you get a structural error in ESSE3, re-export as a standard PDF from Adobe Acrobat or LibreOffice.

CINECA and similar consortium platforms used for PhD applications at Italian research universities typically allow between 2MB and 10MB per document. Research proposal documents in PDF format with embedded references and citations can reach this limit quickly if exported at high quality from LaTeX or Word.

Italian Government Scholarship applications handled through the Italian Ministry of Foreign Affairs use a dedicated portal with a 3MB limit per document. The application requires a passport copy, academic transcripts, and motivation letter, each uploaded separately. Processing all three documents to be clearly under 3MB each is straightforward with grayscale scanning for the passport and transcript, and a clean PDF export for the motivation letter.

Spanish university portals and government systems

Spain's higher education application system is decentralised across autonomous community systems, which means limits can vary significantly depending on which region your target university is in.

Sede Electronica del Ministerio de Educacion is the Spanish national education ministry portal used for official academic records and qualification recognition. Individual document uploads are limited to 10MB. Files must be in PDF format and cannot be password protected. The system requires an electronic ID or digital certificate for authentication in most submission types.

UNED (Universidad Nacional de Educacion a Distancia) uses its own application portal with a 5MB limit per file. International applicants who do not yet have Spanish academic credentials are directed to specific international admission workflows that have their own upload fields with separate limits.

Autonomous community systems vary widely. Catalonia uses a regional platform with limits typically between 2MB and 5MB. Madrid's community system generally allows up to 10MB. Andalusia and Valencia have their own systems with similar range. When in doubt about a specific institution, the institution's international student office will have the exact technical requirements.

Erasmus+ applications for Spanish host institutions go through the Beneficiary Module platform used across the EU. Individual document uploads in this system are limited to 10MB. The system requires specific document types including the online Learning Agreement, which is generated within the platform itself.

Scholarship portals used by Spanish and Italian applicants

DAAD (German Academic Exchange Service) applications from Spain and Italy go through the DAAD portal with a 5MB limit per document. DAAD requires documents in PDF format and does not accept scans submitted as image files. If your university transcripts are only available as physical documents, you must scan them as PDF rather than as JPEG.

Chevening Scholarships for UK study are administered through an online application portal that allows documents up to 20MB. This is more generous than most EU systems, but transcripts and degree certificates must still be official-looking documents that are clearly legible at normal viewing zoom.

La Caixa Fellowship Programme is a major Spanish private scholarship programme that uses its own application portal. The portal accepts PDF files up to 5MB per attachment. The application requires academic transcripts, a research proposal, and letters of recommendation, each uploaded through separate fields.

Fundacion Ramon Areces scholarship applications require PDF documents with a 3MB limit. This programme funds doctoral and postdoctoral researchers and receives applications in Spanish from all nationalities.

Preparing documents for Spanish and Italian portals: practical guidance

Most rejections on these portals come from a small number of recurring issues. Addressing these specifically will resolve the majority of problems.

Scanned documents from non-European universities are often scanned in full colour at high resolution, producing files that are far larger than the portal limit. For any scanned academic document, switching to grayscale at 150 DPI typically reduces the file size by 70 to 85 percent while maintaining full legibility. A transcript that was 8MB in full colour at 300 DPI becomes approximately 1MB in grayscale at 150 DPI.

Official translations of academic documents are sometimes provided as scanned PDFs of physical certified translation documents. These follow the same optimisation rules as any other scan. Grayscale at 150 DPI is appropriate even for certified translations, as the certification stamps and signatures remain visible at this resolution.

Motivation letters and research proposals written in Word or Google Docs should be exported directly as PDF using the "Save as PDF" or "Download as PDF" function. Do not print to PDF, which converts text to image layers. A clean direct export of a text document should be well under 500KB regardless of page count.

Document format requirements beyond file size

Italian portals frequently require that each document be a separate PDF rather than a combined document. Do not merge your degree certificate, transcript, and ID into a single PDF if the portal has separate upload fields for each. Merged documents uploaded to the wrong field cause manual processing delays.

Spanish portals often require documents to be signed or certified before scanning. Uploading an unsigned version and then trying to submit a signed version later often requires contacting the portal administrator to restart the application. Ensure all required certifications and signatures are present on the physical document before scanning.

Both Italian and Spanish government portals frequently require PDF files to be compliant with the PDF/A archival standard for official records. PDF/A is a subset of the PDF format designed for long-term preservation. Microsoft Word, LibreOffice, and Adobe Acrobat all support exporting to PDF/A. If a portal gives a format error despite your file being a normal PDF, try re-exporting as PDF/A.
